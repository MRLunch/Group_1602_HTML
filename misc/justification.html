Justification

As more digital surveillance tools become widely available, governments can better control crime and gather evidence. Digital surveillance systems have also made great contributions to improve traffic conditions. However, these ever-changing digital surveillance tools are constantly violating people's privacy and may even be abused by some private organizations, such as selling personal data to various companies for more targeted advertisements. For example, if a smart device hears the user talk about buying a new car, this information can be sold to car dealership companies without the permission of the user.
